## 1. Why NAP consistency matters (and the byte-identical rule)

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. Search engines and maps platforms cross-check the business details they find across the web. When the same Name, Address, and Phone show up identically on Google, Bing, Apple, Yelp, industry directories, and the data aggregators that feed them, the engines gain confidence the business is real, established, and located where it claims. That confidence is a direct local-ranking signal and it powers the map pack, "yacht broker near me," and branded searches.

Inconsistent NAP does the opposite. "Suite 164" on one site and "Ste 164" on another, "561-817-1547" versus "(561) 817-1547," or an old address on a stale directory can split the business into duplicate or conflicting records. That dilutes ranking, confuses the maps platforms, and erodes the trust the live GBP has already earned.

**The rule: the NAP must be byte-identical everywhere.** Same words, same order, same punctuation, same formatting of "2401 PGA Blvd, Suite 164." Do not abbreviate "Suite" to "Ste." Do not reformat the phone. Do not swap "Boulevard" for "Blvd" or back. Paste the exact block in Section 3 every single time. The live GBP and the site already match it. Everything we add must match it too.

One more standing rule across all of this: the parent company is **One Water Yacht Group (OWYG)**. Never "OWYS." Never "One Water Yacht Sales." If a field asks for parent or affiliation, it is One Water Yacht Group.
