# BUYER'S REPRESENTATION AGREEMENT

**This Buyer's Representation Agreement** (the "Agreement") is made and entered into as of **[Effective Date]** (the "Effective Date") by and between:

**Broker:** Clark Haley, an individual, doing business as **Haley Yachts**, a licensed Florida yacht and ship broker, member of the International Yacht Brokers Association (IYBA) and part of One Water Yacht Group (OWYG), with offices at 2401 PGA Boulevard, Suite 164, Palm Beach Gardens, Florida 33410 (the "Broker" or "Haley Yachts"); and

**Buyer:** **[Buyer Full Legal Name]**, an individual, with a mailing address at **[Buyer Mailing Address]** (the "Buyer").

The Broker and the Buyer are referred to in this Agreement individually as a "Party" and together as the "Parties."

---

## 1. RECITALS AND PURPOSE

1.1. The Buyer wishes to evaluate and, if the Buyer elects to proceed, to purchase a recreational vessel, specifically a **Manta 42 Catamaran** (the "Vessel"), more particularly described as: **[Vessel Year]** Manta 42 Catamaran, Hull Identification Number (HIN) **[Hull ID Number / HIN]**, currently offered for sale by **[Seller Name]** (the "Seller").

1.2. The Buyer wishes to engage the Broker to represent and assist the Buyer in connection with the evaluation, negotiation, and purchase of the Vessel, and the Broker is willing to provide such services, on the terms and conditions set out in this Agreement.

1.3. The Broker has particular knowledge and experience with Manta catamarans and offers that experience to the Buyer as part of the services described below.

---

## 2. ENGAGEMENT AND SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1. **Engagement.** The Buyer engages the Broker, and the Broker accepts the engagement, to represent the Buyer's interests in connection with the potential purchase of the Vessel, on the terms set out in this Agreement.

2.2. **Services.** Subject to the terms of this Agreement, the Broker agrees to provide the following services (the "Services") in connection with the Buyer's potential purchase of the Vessel:

   (a) **Negotiation and purchase.** Assist the Buyer in the negotiation and purchase of the Vessel, including assisting the Buyer in preparing and submitting an offer, advising the Buyer in good faith on price, terms, and deal structure, and acting as the Buyer's point of contact with the Seller and the Seller's broker, if any.

   (b) **Survey and sea trial attendance.** Attend the marine survey and the sea trial of the Vessel and provide the Buyer with the Broker's observations and expertise specific to Manta catamarans. The Broker's attendance and input are advisory only and are not a substitute for the independent inspection and reporting of a qualified marine surveyor and other professionals (see Section 8).

   (c) **Survey review and post-survey negotiation.** Review the results of the marine survey with the Buyer and, if appropriate, assist the Buyer in any post-survey negotiations with the Seller regarding price, repairs, allowances, or other terms.

   (d) **Documentation and title transfer support.** Assist the Buyer through the documentation and title-transfer process. The Broker will **not** perform the documentation, registration, or closing work himself. Instead, the Broker will recommend a qualified documentation and closing company to handle that work. That company will be engaged by, and will bill, the Buyer directly for its services, and its fees are separate from and in addition to the Broker's compensation under this Agreement.

   (e) **Closing contingency.** The Broker's compensation is fully contingent on a successful closing of the Buyer's purchase of the Vessel. If the Buyer does not purchase the Vessel, for any reason, no fee or commission of any kind is owed to the Broker under this Agreement. (See Section 3 for the full compensation terms.)

   (f) **Post-closing training.** After the closing of the purchase, provide the Buyer with one (1) day of orientation and training aboard the Vessel, at a date and location to be mutually agreed by the Parties within a reasonable time after closing.

   (g) **Ongoing telephone assistance.** Provide ongoing telephone assistance to the Buyer regarding the Vessel, as reasonably needed, following the closing.

2.3. **Scope limits.** The Services are limited to those expressly described in Section 2.2. The Broker is not obligated to provide any service not listed, including but not limited to surveying, vessel repair or refit, financing, insurance placement, crewing, delivery, or legal, tax, or accounting advice.

---

## 3. COMPENSATION

3.1. **Fee.** As full compensation for the Services, the Buyer agrees to pay the Broker a fee equal to **two and one-half percent (2.5%) of the final purchase price** of the Vessel (the "Fee").

3.2. **Earned and payable only on closing.** The Fee is earned and payable **only upon the successful closing of the Buyer's purchase of the Vessel**. The Fee is due and paid **at closing**.

3.3. **No closing, no fee.** If the Buyer does not purchase the Vessel, for any reason whatsoever (including but not limited to the Buyer's decision not to proceed, survey or sea-trial results, failure to agree on terms, the Seller's withdrawal, or failure of any condition), then no Fee and no commission, charge, or compensation of any kind is owed to the Broker under this Agreement.

3.4. **No retainer.** No retainer, deposit, or up-front payment is required from the Buyer, and none is being collected under this Agreement.

3.5. **Collection at closing.** The Parties intend that the Fee be paid to the Broker at closing out of the closing proceeds. The Buyer authorizes and directs the closing or documentation company handling the transaction to disburse the Fee to the Broker from the funds at closing, and to reflect the Fee on the closing statement, as a condition of closing. If for any reason the Fee is not disbursed at closing, the Buyer remains responsible for paying the Fee to the Broker promptly upon closing.

3.6. **Third-party costs are separate.** The Fee does not include, and the Buyer is solely responsible for, all third-party costs of the transaction, including but not limited to the marine survey, sea-trial costs, haul-out, documentation and closing company fees, registration and titling fees, taxes, financing costs, insurance, and any repairs.

3.7. **No additional broker charges.** The Broker will not bill the Buyer for any expenses, costs, or charges of any kind in connection with the Services, including the Broker's own travel to attend the survey or sea trial. The only amount the Buyer owes the Broker under this Agreement is the Fee. The third-party transaction costs described in Section 3.6 remain the Buyer's responsibility and are not charged or marked up by the Broker.

---

## 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1. **Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until the earlier of (a) the closing of the Buyer's purchase of the Vessel, (b) **[Agreement Expiration Date]**, or (c) termination by either Party under Section 4.2 (the "Term"). This Agreement is limited to the transaction described in Section 1 and does not create a general or open-ended brokerage relationship.

4.2. **Termination.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time before closing by giving written notice to the other Party. Termination does not relieve the Buyer of the obligation to pay the Fee if a closing of the Vessel later occurs in the circumstances described in Section 5 (Exclusivity and Protection Period).

4.3. **Effect of termination.** On termination, the Broker will stop providing Services. Because the Fee is fully contingent on a successful closing, no Fee is owed on termination unless and until a qualifying closing occurs.

4.4. **Survival.** Sections 3 (to the extent a Fee has been earned), 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13 survive termination or expiration of this Agreement.

---

## 5. EXCLUSIVITY AND PROTECTION PERIOD

5.1. **Exclusive representation.** During the Term, the Broker is the Buyer's sole and exclusive representative for the purchase of the Vessel. The Buyer agrees to work only through the Broker in connection with the Vessel and to refer all communications regarding the Vessel to the Broker.

5.2. **This Vessel only.** This Agreement, and the Fee, apply only to the Buyer's purchase of the specific Vessel described in Section 1. This Agreement does not cover, and no Fee is owed in connection with, the Buyer's purchase of any other vessel.

5.3. **Protection period.** If, within ninety (90) days after the expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Buyer purchases the Vessel, the Fee remains due to the Broker on the same terms as if the closing had occurred during the Term. This protects the Broker against a purchase of the Vessel that is delayed past the Term to avoid the Fee.

---

## 6. BUYER'S RESPONSIBILITIES AND ACKNOWLEDGMENTS

6.1. The Buyer agrees to:

   (a) cooperate in good faith with the Broker and provide accurate and complete information needed to carry out the Services;

   (b) make the Buyer's own decisions regarding whether to make an offer, the offer price and terms, whether to proceed after survey and sea trial, and whether to close;

   (c) engage and pay, directly, the Buyer's own qualified professionals, including a marine surveyor and the recommended documentation and closing company, and any attorney, accountant, financing source, or insurer the Buyer chooses to use;

   (d) be responsible for the deposit, purchase price, taxes, and all closing and third-party costs of the transaction; and

   (e) review and sign the purchase and sale agreement and all closing documents in the Buyer's own judgment.

6.2. The Buyer acknowledges that:

   (a) the Broker represents the Buyer's interests in this transaction;

   (b) the Broker's compensation is fully contingent on a successful closing and is not owed if no purchase closes;

   (c) the documentation, registration, and closing work will be performed by a separate, third-party company that the Buyer engages and pays directly; and

   (d) the Buyer has had the opportunity to review this Agreement with the Buyer's own attorney before signing.

## 7. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR RELATIONSHIP

7.1. The Broker is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties beyond the limited representation described in this Agreement. Neither Party may bind the other except as expressly stated in this Agreement.

## 8. INDEPENDENT PROFESSIONAL SERVICES DISCLAIMER

8.1. The Broker is a licensed Florida yacht and ship broker. The Broker is **not** a marine surveyor, naval architect, marine engineer, mechanic, attorney, accountant, tax adviser, or documentation or title agent, and does not provide services in any of those capacities under this Agreement.

8.2. The Broker's attendance at the survey and sea trial, and any observations, opinions, or input the Broker provides (including input specific to Manta catamarans), are **advisory only**. They are not a survey, inspection, certification, valuation, or professional opinion, and the Buyer must not rely on them as such.

8.3. The Buyer is solely responsible for, and must rely on, the Buyer's own independent professionals for the condition, value, seaworthiness, and fitness of the Vessel and for the legal, tax, financing, insurance, documentation, and title aspects of the transaction. This includes an independent marine survey and sea trial, and independent legal, tax, accounting, documentation, and title advice, all obtained at the Buyer's own expense.

8.4. The documentation, registration, title transfer, and closing are handled by a separate third-party company that the Buyer engages and that bills the Buyer directly. The Broker's recommendation of such a company is a convenience only and is not a guarantee or endorsement of that company's work.

## 9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY; NO GUARANTEE

9.1. **No guarantee.** The Broker does not guarantee the condition, quality, value, seaworthiness, or fitness for any purpose of the Vessel, the accuracy of any information provided by the Seller or third parties, or any particular outcome of the transaction, including that the Buyer will be able to purchase the Vessel or purchase it on any particular terms.

9.2. **Limitation.** To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Broker's total liability to the Buyer arising out of or relating to this Agreement or the Services, regardless of the form of action, will not exceed the amount of the Fee actually paid to the Broker under this Agreement. The Broker is not liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages.

9.3. The Buyer's remedies and the Broker's liability are limited as set out in this Section 9, except to the extent any such limitation is not permitted by applicable Florida law.

## 10. CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1. Each Party agrees to keep confidential the non-public information of the other Party that it learns in connection with this transaction, including the Buyer's identity, financial information, and the terms of the transaction, and to use such information only for the purpose of carrying out this Agreement. This Section does not apply to information that is or becomes public through no fault of the receiving Party, that is required to be disclosed by law or to complete the transaction (including disclosure to the Seller, the closing company, lenders, insurers, and professional advisers as reasonably necessary), or that a Party already lawfully possesses.

## 11. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

11.1. **Governing law.** This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Florida, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

11.2. **Venue.** The Parties agree that the exclusive venue for any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement is Palm Beach County, Florida.

11.3. **Dispute resolution.** Before commencing any other proceeding, the Parties will first attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement through mediation in Palm Beach County, Florida, before a mutually agreed mediator, with the mediator's fee shared equally by the Parties. If the dispute is not resolved through mediation within thirty (30) days after a Party first requests mediation in writing, either Party may then pursue the dispute in the state or federal courts located in Palm Beach County, Florida.

11.4. **Attorneys' fees.** In any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the prevailing Party is entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs, including those incurred in mediation, at trial, and on any appeal.
