Letter of Understanding

This letter will outline the general terms of employment of 10 Year Plan, Inc., (Captain Clark”) by Colonel Brashier, Nordhavn 41, Name TBD. Captain Clark will be hired as an independent contractor/captain at a rate of $450.00 US/per day, $2,800.00 US/per week, (7 consecutive days), and/or monthly at $12,000.00 US/per 30 day month. All amounts shall be paid in U.S. Dollars. Employment is scheduled for (Date TBD), and will actually commence when Captain Clark leaves his FL residence to begin the trip to Nordhavn 41, Name TBD. It will terminate when Captain Clark returns to his FL residence. For purpose of this Letter of Understanding, everyone agrees that any portion of a day shall be considered a whole day.

During his employment, Captain Clark’s room, board, laundry and all other reasonable and customary expenses will be provided by Colonel Brashier. Colonel Brashier shall also pay Captain Clark’s reasonable expenses for roundtrip travel from his home in Jupiter, FL to the Nordhavn 41, Name TBD including coach class airline ticket, ground transportation e.g., train, rental car costs (and those costs associated with a rental car), and/or shuttle/taxi service, meals, and lodging required to complete his journey back to his FL residence. There are no transportation charges for travel within 15 miles of Captain Clark’s residence. While traveling further than 15 miles there will be a charge of $0.50/mile to cover transportation costs.

Compensation for Captain Clark will be paid upon completion of engagement by a method of wire transfer into his checking account, written check, or credit card, whichever is easier.

Payment shall be due upon receipt of billing statement, unless otherwise agreed to in writing, in advance.

It is the goal of each of us to have a good working and rewarding relationship, with a heavy emphasis on communication. Captain Clark will ensure that the vessel is properly operated and that all individuals aboard are safe and as reasonably comfortable as possible, considering the conditions. Colonel Brashier will ensure that all USCG required safety gear and publications are aboard, all in good working order, and within their expiration dates.

If, at any time, and for any reason, either party feels that this arrangement is not working out as intended, we each agree to make our best efforts to resolve our differences. If this cannot be accomplished, either party may terminate this agreement by giving the other party at least 7 days’ notice of their desire to terminate. Everyone understands that safety is of primary importance and that sufficient notice is required to ensure that the boat is in a desirable location.

I very much look forward to working with/for you aboard your Nordhavn 41, Name TBD. Please feel free to contact me anytime if I can be of assistance in preparing for our training session in any way. It is my goal to prepare you (both) to handle and operate your boat safely and confidently for the new adventures that lie ahead. This is the best part about cruising on your own boat, to see the places you may have only heard about or seen pictures of, to spend a relaxing time in one of your favorite locations you may have had to rush through as a tourist, and to experience a whole new way of life and living few people have the means, privilege, and most importantly the courage to experience.

I hope you find this proposal fair and equitable and I look forward to your input.

If you are in agreement with this Letter of Understanding, I would appreciate your signing the same and returning a copy to me by return email.

Best regards,

Captain Clark Haley,

Ten Year Plan Investments, LTD
